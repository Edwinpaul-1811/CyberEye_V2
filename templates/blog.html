Understanding Deepfakes

Deepfakes are synthetic media in which a person in an existing image or video is replaced with someone else's likeness using artificial intelligence. The term comes from a combination of "deep learning" and "fake." This technology can create incredibly realistic—but entirely fabricated—content that is difficult to distinguish from real media.

Why Detect Deepfakes?

While deepfakes have legitimate uses in entertainment and accessibility, they also pose serious threats to:

  • Privacy and Identity: Deepfakes can impersonate someone without their consent.
  • Politics and Misinformation: Fake videos of public figures can spread false narratives.
  • Cybersecurity: Deepfakes are used in fraud and social engineering attacks.

How to Avoid Being Fooled

Here are a few ways to protect yourself from being misled by deepfakes:

  • Be skeptical of videos with unusual lighting, unnatural blinking, or robotic movements.
  • Cross-check with trusted sources and official statements.
  • Use AI detection tools like Cyber Eye to analyze suspicious content.
